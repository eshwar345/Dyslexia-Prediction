The Stages of Dyslexia

1. Early Development (Ages 3-8)

Signs: Delayed speech, difficulty with phonemic awareness (rhyming, recognizing sounds), and trouble learning new words or the alphabet.

Behavior: Avoidance of reading activities, frustration with language tasks, and confusion with similar-looking letters (like b and d).

2. School Age (Ages 9-12)

Signs: Ongoing struggles with reading fluency, comprehension, and spelling. Reading may become slow and labor-intensive.

Behavior: Increased anxiety about academic performance, lower self-esteem, and potential avoidance of reading aloud or participating in class discussions.

3. Adolescence to Adulthood (Ages 13+)

Signs: Continued difficulties with complex texts and writing tasks. Individuals may rely on compensatory strategies like audio books and assistive technology.

Behavior: May choose careers or educational paths that minimize reading requirements, seek accommodations in work or school, and develop effective coping strategies to manage challenges.
